Understanding Contexto

Contexto is a daily word puzzle that uses artificial intelligence to rank words by their contextual similarity to a hidden target word. When you guess a word, the game tells you how close your guess is to the answer based on how similarly the words are used in context — not their spelling. This means a word like "dog" might be very close to "puppy" even though they share no letters. The Contexto daily puzzle resets every day, offering a fresh semantic challenge.

Learning how to play Contexto is different from traditional word games. There are no color-coded letter clues — instead, you receive a numerical rank for each guess. The lower the rank number, the closer your guess is to the target word in terms of meaning. Contexto rules allow unlimited guesses, but the challenge is to find the answer in as few guesses as possible. The game relies on your understanding of word associations, categories, and semantic relationships.

Tips for Improving Your Contexto Scores

Start with category words. A proven Contexto strategy is to begin with broad category words like "animal," "food," "place," or "feeling." These guesses help you quickly identify the semantic neighborhood of the target word. Once you know the general category, you can drill down with more specific guesses.

Follow the rank feedback. Contexto tips from top players emphasize paying close attention to the rank numbers. When a guess has a low rank (close to #1), explore words in the same semantic field. When a guess has a high rank, move to a completely different category. This systematic approach helps you converge on the answer efficiently.

Build semantic associations. Learning how to get better at Contexto involves strengthening your understanding of word relationships. Read widely, explore synonyms, and practice thinking about how words connect conceptually. The more robust your semantic network, the faster you'll find the target word each day.
