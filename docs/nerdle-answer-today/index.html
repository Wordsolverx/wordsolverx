Understanding Nerdle

Nerdle is a daily math puzzle game where players must guess a hidden mathematical equation within six attempts. Instead of guessing a five-letter word like in Wordle, you guess an eight-character equation that includes numbers and mathematical operators (plus, minus, multiply, divide, equals). After each guess, the game provides color-coded feedback — green means the character is correct and in the right position, purple means the character is in the equation but in the wrong position, and black means the character isn't in the equation at all. The Nerdle daily puzzle resets every 24 hours.

Learning how to play Nerdle requires a slightly different mindset than Wordle. You need to think about mathematical operations and how numbers combine to form valid equations. Nerdle rules state that each guess must be a valid mathematical equation with an equals sign, and the calculation must be correct. For example, "12+35=47" is a valid guess. Commutative equations are accepted (so "5+7=12" and "7+5=12" would both be green), making the game more forgiving in certain situations.

Tips for Improving Your Nerdle Scores

Start with information-rich guesses. A smart Nerdle strategy involves opening with equations that test multiple operations and a wide range of numbers. A good first guess like "12+35=47" tests addition, uses several different digits, and covers multiple positions. This helps you quickly identify which numbers and operations are in the equation.
