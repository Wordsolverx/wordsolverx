Understanding Framed

Framed is a daily movie guessing game where players must identify a film based on a sequence of still frames extracted from the movie. You start with a single frame, and for each incorrect guess, an additional frame is revealed to provide more context. The Framed daily puzzle features movies from a wide range of genres and eras, from classic cinema to recent blockbusters. It's a must-play for movie lovers who enjoy visual storytelling and film trivia.

Learning how to play Framed is simple but becoming skilled requires broad movie knowledge. You start by examining the first frame carefully, looking for visual clues like actors, settings, color grading, costumes, and cinematography style. Framed rules give you up to six guesses, with a new frame revealed after each incorrect attempt. The fewer frames you need, the better your score — making each correct early guess a point of pride.
