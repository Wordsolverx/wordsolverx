## Understanding Waffle

Waffle is a daily word puzzle where players are presented with a grid of letters arranged in a waffle-shaped pattern (a cross-like grid) that contains several interlocking words. The letters are jumbled, and your task is to swap them around until all the words — both across and down — are correct. Green letters are already in the correct position, yellow letters belong in the word but are in the wrong spot, and gray letters need to be moved to a different word entirely. The Waffle daily puzzle resets every 24 hours, giving players a fresh grid to solve each day.

Learning how to play Waffle is different from Wordle because you're not guessing words — you're rearranging letters that are already on the board. Waffle rules give you a limited number of swaps to solve the entire grid, so you need to be strategic about which letters you move and where. The challenge lies in figuring out which words are formed by the intersecting rows and columns, and then efficiently swapping letters to their correct positions. Each correct word contributes to solving the others, since the words share letters at their intersection points.
