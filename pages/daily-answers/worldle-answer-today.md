## Understanding Worldle

Worldle is a daily geography game where players must guess a mystery country based on its outline or silhouette. After each incorrect guess, the game provides feedback including the distance to the target country, the direction you need to move, and a proximity percentage. The closer your guess is geographically, the higher the proximity percentage. The Worldle daily puzzle resets every 24 hours, giving players a new country outline to identify each day. It's a brilliant test of geographic knowledge and spatial reasoning.

Learning how to play Worldle is simple but mastering it requires extensive knowledge of world geography and country shapes. You start by examining the outline of the mystery country and making your best guess. Worldle rules allow six guesses, and after each incorrect attempt, you receive the distance in kilometers, a directional arrow, and a proximity percentage that tells you how close you are. The challenge lies in recognizing country shapes — some are instantly recognizable (like Italy's boot), while others can be quite tricky.
