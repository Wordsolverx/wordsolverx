## Understanding Wordle

Wordle is the original daily word guessing game that took the internet by storm. Created by Josh Wardle and later acquired by The New York Times, Wordle challenges players to guess a five-letter word in six attempts. After each guess, the game provides color-coded feedback: green tiles indicate a correct letter in the correct position, yellow tiles indicate a correct letter in the wrong position, and gray tiles indicate a letter that is not in the word at all. The Wordle daily puzzle resets every 24 hours at midnight, giving players around the world a fresh word to discover each day.

Learning how to play Wordle is simple, but mastering it requires vocabulary knowledge, strategic thinking, and efficient use of feedback. Wordle rules are straightforward: you have six guesses to find the hidden five-letter word. Each guess must be a valid English word, and the feedback from each guess helps you narrow down the possibilities. The key is using each guess to extract maximum information, eliminating possibilities systematically until you identify the correct word. Today's Wordle is always a common English word — never a proper noun or an obscure term.

## Tips for Improving Your Wordle Scores

**Use an optimal starting word.** A proven Wordle strategy begins with a word that maximizes information gain. Data analysis shows that words like "CRANE," "SLATE," "TRACE," and "RAISE" are among the best starting words because they test multiple common vowels and consonants simultaneously. Your first guess sets the foundation for the entire puzzle, so choose strategically rather than randomly.

**Think about letter frequency.** Wordle tips from expert players emphasize understanding which letters appear most frequently in English words and in Wordle answers specifically. E, A, R, O, T, L, I, S, and N are the most common letters, so prioritizing these in your early guesses helps you narrow down the answer more quickly. Avoid wasting guesses on uncommon letters like Q, X, Z, and J unless you have a specific reason to test them.

**Use the process of elimination.** Learning how to get better at Wordle involves systematically using each guess to eliminate possibilities. Gray letters are just as valuable as green and yellow ones — they tell you what's NOT in the word, which dramatically reduces the remaining options. Keep a mental (or physical) list of eliminated letters and use it to make more targeted guesses as the puzzle progresses. With practice, you'll develop an intuitive sense for which words are most likely based on the feedback pattern.
